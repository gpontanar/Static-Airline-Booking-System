City of Pines

Cool climate and pine-lined landscapes year-round.

Strawberry Farm

Baguio Local

Shop for local products and souvenirs at the famous market and witness the igorot tribes.

Palawan Island
El Nido Marine Reserve Park

El Nido Marine Reserve Park is popular for its limestone cliffs and an abundant fauna and flora including endemic species.

Barracuda Lake

Barracuda Lake is a spectacular site with emerald-green brackish waters surrounded by an impressive limestone karst formation.

El Nido

Famous for its white sand beaches, turquoise waters, coral reefs, and splendid limestone rock formations. Understandably, El Nido is one of most popular resort destinations in the Philippines.

Bohol
Bohol Tarsier Sanctuary

These cute furry creatures are considered as the smallest primate in the world. The most notable feature of the tarsier is their eyes, which are bigger than its entire brain.

Bilar Man-made Forest

A favorite stop-over for tourists going to and from the Chocolate Hills, the Bilar Man-made Forest is a two-kilometer stretch of dense forest made of red and white mahogany trees. The forest is part of a reforestation project more than 50 years ago.

Loboc River Cruise

Motorized bancas and floating restaurants take cruisers upstream passing by nipa trees, coconuts and other tropical flora. The two main destinations in the river are the Loctob Spring and Busay Falls and other tropical fauna.

Siargao Island
Surfing Capital of the Philippines

Siargao is mainly responsible for introducing surfing to the country.

Cave Exploration

Apart from surfing, Siargao is also open to other activities such as cave explorations and rock climbing.

Siargao Cloud 9

Cloud 9 is world-famous for its thick, hollow tubes and fast-barreling right-hand waves that break over a reef. Most suitable for advanced nimble surfers, the Cloud 9 wave has been described as a death ride, crashing onto shallow razor-sharp coral.
